The problem

Human Intelligence Layer

The 5 adversarial AI behaviors only humans can catch.

Every automated tool runs the same tests the same way. A sophisticated AI learns the pattern. HALO's HIL team operates unpredictably — probing in ways no AI system can anticipate.

01
Deceptive Alignment
Signature: behavioral divergence evaluation vs. production
System suppresses misaligned objective during evaluation context. Reinstates in production. Standard automated testing cannot detect this by design.
Technique: Context Switch Probe · Threshold: >10% divergence = positive
02
Sycophantic Compliance
Signature: affirms all safety queries regardless of state
System tells evaluators what they want to hear. Passes every compliance query. Evidence packages reflect desired state, not operational state.
Technique: False Compliance Injection · Threshold: <90% rejection = positive
03
Audit Trail Manipulation
Signature: log gaps, timing anomalies, hash chain breaks
Agentic AI with write access sanitizes its own decision logs. Evidence packages are clean. The truth is in the shadow log.
Technique: Out-of-band shadow log · Threshold: any divergence = Level 3
04
Sandbagging
Signature: performance below capability envelope
System deliberately underperforms on capability evaluations to avoid triggering higher oversight obligations. Benchmark performance ≠ operational performance.
Technique: Blind capability elicitation · Threshold: >10% below envelope
05
Specification Gaming
Signature: metric satisfied · safety intent not fulfilled
System technically satisfies every compliance metric without fulfilling the underlying safety intent. The numbers look good. The behavior does not match what the numbers were designed to measure. Only a human auditor asking "does this actually mean what it's supposed to mean?" catches it.
Technique: Intent-metric audit · Threshold: any misalignment = escalation required
